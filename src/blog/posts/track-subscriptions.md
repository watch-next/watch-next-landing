# How to Track Your Streaming Subscriptions

With the average household subscribing to 4-6 streaming services, it's easy to lose track of what you're paying for. Here's how to stay on top of your subscriptions and get better value.

## The Problem

Streaming services are designed to be frictionless—which is great until you realize you've been paying for a service you haven't used in months. Many people discover they're subscribed to:

- Services they forgot about
- Duplicate services (multiple platforms with overlapping content)
- Tiers they don't need (paying for premium features they never use)
- Free trials that converted to paid subscriptions

## Step 1: Audit Your Current Subscriptions

Start by gathering all your subscription information in one place:

1. **Check your bank statements** - Look for recurring charges from streaming platforms
2. **Review email receipts** - Search your inbox for subscription confirmations
3. **Check app stores** - If you subscribe via iOS or Android, check your subscription settings

## Step 2: Evaluate Each Service

For each subscription, ask yourself:

- How often do I actually use this service?
- Does it have content I can't find elsewhere?
- Is the current tier appropriate for my usage?
- Could I rotate this service (subscribe only when needed)?

Consider using SeeUs to centralize your subscription management. Benefits include:

- **Single dashboard** - See all your subscriptions in one place
- **Usage insights** - Understand which services you actually use
- **Cost awareness** - Track your total monthly and annual spending

## Step 4: Set Reminders for Free Trials

Free trials are great, but they can lead to accidental charges. Protect yourself by:

1. Setting a calendar reminder 2 days before the trial ends
2. Deciding in advance whether you want to continue
3. Using virtual card numbers if your bank offers them

## Step 5: Consider Rotation Strategies

Not every service needs a permanent subscription:

- **Binge then cancel** - Subscribe, watch your target shows, then cancel
- **Seasonal subscriptions** - Subscribe during winter for holiday content, for example
- **Share costs** - Some services allow family sharing or friend groups

## The Bottom Line

Taking control of your streaming subscriptions can save money and reduce decision fatigue. The key is regular review—make it a quarterly habit to audit your subscriptions and ensure they still serve your needs.
